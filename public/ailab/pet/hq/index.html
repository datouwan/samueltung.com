02 · Roadmap

Phases and their gates

Startup discipline for a hardware pet: every phase has a kill-or-continue gate, and each phase is roughly 10× cheaper than the one after it. Fail early, fail cheap.

  • Concept dossier done · Jul 2026 Species design, market study, component architecture — the three sibling pages of this hub. Gate passed: a coherent, differentiated concept with defensible unit economics on paper.
  • Phase 0 — Illusion test next Wizard-of-Oz puppet: fur shell, fronds, warmth, purr — a human driving the "mind." Show it to strangers. Gate: do people treat it as alive and ask to keep it? Budget: a few $k, before any tooling is cut.
  • Phase 1 — Works-like breadboard Fronds, glide base, warmth, purr, and the instinct layer running on real electronics — ugly is fine. Gate: the frond language is readable without explanation; comprehension moments land ("sulks on your suitcase").
  • Phase 2 — Looks-like alphas in homes A handful of hand-built units living with real families for 90+ days. Gate: retention past month 3 — the metric that killed the category graveyard (Aibo, Jibo, Vector, Cozmo).
  • Phase 3 — EVT / DVT / certification Tooling, molds, FCC/CE. The ~$2–4 M NRE check gets written here and nowhere earlier. Gate: landed COGS ≤ $260 at 20k/yr volume; safety and privacy architecture audited.
  • Phase 4 — D2C launch Direct-to-consumer first (the Lovot playbook), subscription attached from day one. Gate: the north star — 30–40k units/yr or >40% attach = a profitable company.
